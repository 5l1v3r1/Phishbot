Choosing a Good Forklift Trainer

There is no doubt that forklifts are some of the most important equipment in modern industries. Also known as the lift truck, it greatly improves the efficiency and enhances productivity in manufacturing firms that require the movement of raw material and parts through various phases of the production process. This powered industrial vehicle is also indispensable in modern warehouse operations and greatly enhances stacking capability thus optimizing on the use of space. That over one million forklifts were sold this year is a clear indication of its popularity around the world.

Safety is undoubtedly one of the major challenges when working with a forklift. It is therefore essential that operators are well versed with not just operational procedures but also safety considerations. Forklift owners should ensure that the forklift trainers they select to instruct their staff understand safety precautions extremely well. A competent forklift trainer can help you avoid costly and dangerous accidents. Forklifts can lift amazingly heavy loads and hoist them to great heights. This means that mishandling more than a ton of material while raised several feet high can be disastrous. Following the right procedure during forklift operations is therefore crucial.

The importance of choosing the right forklift trainer the trainer program cannot be overemphasized. The skills he or she imparts to the operator will encourage better productivity and promote safety at the workplace. However, this is only possible if the forklift trainer is well versed with the pertinent skills. As an employer looking for a good trainer, documentation should be your first major consideration. Trainer certification should comply with all OSHA requirements. References and relevant experience should also be factored in. Good forklift trainers should be familiar with a wide variety of forklifts to give better exposure to your operators.

Forklift accidents can leave their victims with debilitating injuries and many are even fatal. In the year 2014, a number of gruesome forklift accidents have been recorded around the country.

  1. A customer in a Lynwood based store was crushed to death when a forklift operator accidentally dropped a massive load on him in the parking area.
  2. In October, a worker in a transportation company who had only worked for two weeks also died after she was crushed by a falling load.

Although less than 100 forklift accidents are recorded annually, it is believed that this number can be whittled down even further.

Various safety measures can be put in place to avoid the occurrence of unnecessary forklift accidents. They include but are not limited to

  • Regulating speed. The average forklift carries about three tons. With such a load, the operator should maintain a low speed which should not exceed 3 mph. This will enable one to react to any contingency in time.
  • Avoid overloading. Often, the need to accomplish tasks faster leads to operators carrying more loads than the forklift is designed to carry. However, this is extremely dangerous since most accidents resulting from tipping can be attributed to this.
  • Maintenance. Most forklift accidents are attributed to human error. However, when regular maintenance is not carried out, faulty equipment can malfunction and cause accidents.
