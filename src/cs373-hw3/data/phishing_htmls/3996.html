Newborn Boy Bow Tie Prop Tutorial

2 Jun
June 2, 2013

I just love my handmade Newborn Boy Bow Tie Props! They are so cute and can even be used on older siblings for photos. These are super easy to make, require very minimal amount of material, and are hand-sewn (no machine required!) Here is the tutorial on how to make these simple newborn photography props: (Photos shown first, and then detailed instructions listed out below)

1) Cut a piece of paper in to two pieces for your pattern. 1 size will be 9 1/2 inches wide by 3 1/2 inches high. The other piece will wrap around the center of the bow and that will be a 2 1/4 inch by 2 1/4 square. Now gather your coordinating scraps of material and cut out your bow tie pieces. Does not use up much material so quarter flats will be more than enough material.

2) Take bow tie piece and fold in edges to center and overlap 1/8 of an inch. Fold it like a hot dog (back to kindergarten days!) Then fold the long edges in to the center and overlap that 3/4 of an inch. Here is the tricky part of the folding process so see picture for details, fold again like a hot dog in half towards the front of the bow tie and then fold back the raw edges and tack with a pin. It looks like an accordion. It helps to quickly sew this together before you proceed.

3) Take your center coordinating piece and fold that in thirds. Then wrap it around the bow tie, don’t tug it tight just do it so its not loose. Sew together on back side, as shown in picture.

I can’t say I am the best at being a TEACHER, which is why I chose to be a photographer. Hopefully you can make sense of my directions between the instructions and the photos. Also, please note that the tutorial photos were quick snapshots as I didn’t take the time to pull out my DSLR 😉
