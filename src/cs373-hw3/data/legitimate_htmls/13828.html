Jun 11, 2016   Intel discontinues Xeon E7 v2 processors
We already reported this week that Intel launched new Xeon E7 v4 microprocessors for Brickland platform, which uses socket 2011-1. Alongside with introduction of new products, Intel announced discontinuation dates for Xeon E7 v2 server CPUs, also compatible with the socket 2011-1. These Ivy Bridge-based processors were introduced in February 2014. They had up to 15 cores, up to 37.5 MB L3 cache and worked with DDR3 memory.   [Full story]
Site updates

Jul 23, 2016: Latest changes and new additions

There were several additions to the site and one important change, that will affect visitors using adblockers. Let's start with the good news. I added missing Bristol Ridge / Stoney Ridge APUs and started updating all related pages (codenames, family descriptions, etc). Intel processor number pages and AMD model number pages were split so that each manufacturer now has 4 types of pages: desktop, mobile, server and embedded. This reduced the size of pages. Now, the bad news. The time of polite appeals to turn off ad blocking is over. It doesn't work. Now, if your browser or computer blocks ads and you visited 20 or so pages, your access to page content can be blocked. You have been warned. By they way, thanks to 2% of all visitors who actually disabled their adblockers.

Jul 13, 2016: CPU discussion pages added

I added CPU discussion pages for modern microprocessors. For long time, all CPU pages had a simple comment system, that allowed visitors to post corrections and additional information. However, we did not allow certain types of comments, such as CPU upgrade and troubleshooting questions. These can be posted now on CPU discussion pages. The pages also contain brief processor specs and related links. There are two things that make discussion pages different from CPU pages: they do not have ads and there is no pre-moderation when a message is posted (pre-moderation is still required for messages with pictures). As an example, here is an AMD FX-8350 discussion page. Discussion pages for older processors can be enabled by request.

Jun 07, 2016: Limiting access to bots and scrapers

I got tired of a very persistent content scraper (or scrapers), that used open proxies to download our specifications. As a countermeasure, I modified internal structure of CPU pages to show less content to non-visitors. I plan to modify other pages as well. I also temporary changed blocking policy to block all open proxies, and not just abused ones.

May 31, 2016: Website changes coming

I decided to give it a last shot before freezing my work on the site. In the course of the next three months I will introduce a number of features, aimed to add more CPU-related information and allow visitors to contribute short reviews, specifications and benchmarks. Ad-free access will be provided to frequent contributors and chip collectors, as well as available for a fee. Donations will be also accepted. Hopefully, these improvements will increase site's revenue, that will be used to fund all development.   [More]

Jun 10, 2016   New budget A4 Pro-3350B APU is available
New AMD A4 business-class APU, dubbed A4-3350B, was released in May. The processor is based on year-old Carrizo-L design and produced on 28nm manufacturing process. Similar to other "Carrizo-L" A4-series products, the A4-3350B comes with 4 cores, 2 MB L2 cache and Radeon graphics with 128 shader cores. Nonetheless, the APU does offer better performance than older Carrizo-L models, such as A4-7210, due to increased CPU and GPU frequencies.   [Full story]
New CPUs: AMD A4 Pro-3350B
Jun 10, 2016   Intel launched Xeon E7-4800 v4 and E7-8800 v4 server CPUs
Intel launched Xeon E7 v4 microprocessors earlier this week. Codenamed Broadwell-EX, Xeon E7 v4 products are aimed at enterprise and mission-critical applications, and scale up to 4 or 8 sockets depending on SKU. Additionally, third-party vendors are expected to offer systems supporting up to 32 sockets. New in the forth generation Xeon E7 CPUs is more efficient Broadwell microarchitecture, more cores and support for more memory.   [Full story]

May 11, 2016   Unreleased Celeron, Pentium and Core i3 desktop CPUs spotted
Earlier this week, Intel published a Product Change Notification (PCN) document that revealed several upcoming Celeron, Pentium and Core i3 processors. They are likely to be launched during next Skylake refresh. New Core i3 CPUs are Core i3-6120, i3-6120T and i3-6320T. Pentium family will be refreshed with G4420, G4420T, G4520T and G4540 SKUs. There will be also two Celeron SKUs, G3920T and G3940.   [Full story]
May 02, 2016   Embedded Pentium D and Xeon D CPUs released
In the first quarter of 2016, Intel expanded Xeon D and Pentium lineups with new "Broadwell-DE" models for microservers and communications markets. New features of these CPUs were increased number of cores, up to 12 and 16 on some SKUs, and larger size of L3 cache. In April, Intel released embedded D1519, D-1529, D-1539 and D-1559 models with the number of cores ranging from 4 to 12. Unlike other Pentium D and Xeon D family members, designed for commercial temperature range, the D-15x9 processors can operate in extended temperature range from -40°C to +85°C.   [Full story]
New CPUs: Intel Pentium D1519, Intel Xeon D-1529, Intel Xeon D-1539, Intel Xeon D-1559
Mar 20, 2016   Intel quietly launched Core i7-6660U processor
New Intel mobile microprocessor has been added to Intel's pricelist. The newly released CPU, which belongs to Skylake-U class of processors with low or ultra low power consumption, has a model number of i7-6660U. The processor replaced Core i7-6650U, that was available in laptop PCs since last September. The Core i7-6660U offers 200 MHz higher operating frequency at the same price level as the 6650U.   [Full story]
New CPUs: Intel Core i7-6660U
Mar 01, 2016   AMD announces A10-7890K APU and Athlon X4 880K CPU
AMD today announced two new flagship products for socket FM2+ platform, an A10-7890K and Athlon X4 880K. The A10-7890K Accelerated Processing Unit runs at 4 GHz, or 200 MHz faster than its predecessor, and it comes with low-noise Wraith Cooler, rated at 125 Watt. The Athlon X4 880K operates at the same base speed and does not have integrated graphics. This processor is also shipped with "quiet" thermal solution.   [Full story]
Feb 24, 2016   Pre-order prices of Intel Xeon E5-2600 v4 processors
Upcoming Xeon E5-2600 v4 series microprocessors are now available for pre-order in US. The forth generation Xeon E5-2600 series, codenamed "Broadwell-EP", is a refresh of Grantley (LGA2011-3) platform, used in single-, dual- and quad-socket server systems. New Xeons are expected to incorporate a number of enhancements, such as smaller manufacturing process, optimized microarchitecture, increased core count, larger last level cache, Supervisor mode access prevention feature, and support for DDR4-2400 memory.   [Full story]
