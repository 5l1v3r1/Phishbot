Destination of the Month

Saint Petersburg

This beautiful city is a world-class destination and one of Russia's largest. A historic center threaded with canals and dotted with baroque bridges. Check out more on our Destination of the Month.

Off the Beaten Path

Canaima National Park

It's every bit as striking as it looks. This park is home to the tallest waterfall on Earth, Angel Falls. The park itself is rich with wildlife, lagoons, and greenery. Learn more about it by reading the guide!

Landmark of the Month

Golden Gate Bridge

Many consider the Golden Gate Bridge as San Francisco's crown jewel. This gorgeous landmark is instantly recognizable and provides sweeping views of the bay and the Pacific. Learn more about the bridge by reading the guide.

Language of the Month

Cantonese

Cantonese is a widely spoken Chinese language. You're sure to hear it in Hong Kong, Guangdong, Kuala Lumpur, and various Chinatowns across the globe. Check out our guide on the language.

Itinerary

Narrow Road

This itinerary is based on the famed haiku poet Matsuo Basho's most famous work, Narrow Road to the Deep North, a poem-filled travelogue through Japan's remote northeastern region of Tohoku.

Cuisine of the Month

Moroccan

Moroccan cuisine is often reputed to be some of the best in the world, with countless dishes and variations proudly bearing the country's colonial and Arabic influences.

1. Milan

If Rome represents the "old" Italy, Milan represents the "new" Italy. Milan is the most modern of all Italian cities, and it still keeps most of its past history intact. Between May- October the city will host Expo 2015 that will explore the theme "Feeding the Planet, Energy for Life".

2. Havana

Cosmopolitan capital of Cuba with a swinging nightlife to explore. The announcement that the USA is re-establishing diplomatic relations with Cuba, could well make the island nation a “hot” destination this year.

3. Kotor

Kotor is a coastal town in Montenegro, well-known for its World heritage medieval structures and its stunning natural settings. Montenegro has been gaining a lot of tourism steam over the past decade and will have plenty of events this year (music festivals, boat festival and Kotor Carnival).

4. Singapore

Combining the skyscrapers and subways of a modern city with a medley of Chinese, Malay and Indian influences, this Garden City makes a great stopover or springboard into the region. In 2015 Singapore will celebrate its 50th birthday.

5. Rocky Mountains

The Rocky Mountains are a western region of the USA, and offer skiing in the winter, and road and mountain biking, hiking, camping, kayaking, and white water rafting in the summer. In 2015 the park will celebrate its 100th anniversary.
