Tufts People

Diego Millan, Ph.D. Candidate in English

Diego Millan researches Black laughter in literature by looking at how how laughter plays into the development of Black social consciousness and political action. He plans to adapt his research and dissertation into a book that he hopes will be appealing to those in the fields of literature, American Studies, Black studies, and Humor studies.

Learn More About Diego's Research
Gevvie Stone, M14

Alumna and rower Gevvie Stone qualified for the Olympic Rowing Team and will represent the United States in the 2016 Summer Olympics in Rio. Gevvie competed in the single sculls race at the 2012 Summer Olympics and placed 1st in Final B and 7th overall. 

Learn More About Gevvie's Journey to Rio
Gongga Baerde, A20

Gongga Baerde currently lives in Madrid, Spain as part of Tufts 1+4 bridge-year program. 1+4 provides accepted students the opportunity to learn from a transformational year of full-time community service. In Spain, Gongga is volunteering at an orphanage helping foster children and weight lifting in her free time. 

Read Gongga's Blog About her 1+4 Experience
Alan D. Solomont, Dean of Tisch College of Civic Life

Alan D. Solomont, former U.S. ambassador to Spain and Andorra and current Dean of Tisch College of Civic Life, strives to strengthen the colleges role as a leader in civic engagement, empower students to change their communities and their world, and remain at the frontier of research that informs our national and international policy debates.

Read the Dean’s Article on Civic Life in the Huffington Post
Roberta Jacobson, F86

Fletcher School alumna Roberta Jacobson was recently confirmed to be the US ambassador to Mexico, making her the first women to hold that position. Prior to her new role, Roberta was Assistant Secretary of State for Western Hemisphere Affairs since 2012, and before that she was the State Department’s point person for Mexico, Canada and NAFTA. 

Learn More About Roberta's Career
