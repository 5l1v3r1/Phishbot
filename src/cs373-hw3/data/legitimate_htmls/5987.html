Making a Difference

May 2016 graduate Dawite Ewnetu, a computer science and mathematics major, is returning to his native Ethiopia to build shelters for children who must work to support their families. “They sell items and clean shoes. Sometimes they have no place to sleep at night, or anyplace to store their belongings,” he says. Afterward, he'll return to the United States to a job as a systems analyst at Lockheed Martin.

Going Home and Giving Back

Inclusive and Accessible

When rising sophomore Katherine Quigley volunteered with Mason's Office of Leadership Education and Development (LEAD), she found her calling. She plans to work in special education and advocate for students with disabilities.

Finding a Career in a Volunteer Opportunity

Redefining Excellence

Mason junior biology major Christopher Fernandez received a prestigious Udall Scholarship, which honors the legacy of the late brothers and politicians, Morris and Stewart Udall. The $7,000 award goes to sophomores and juniors committed to matters related to the environment or Native Americans.

Becoming a Steward of the Earth

Located for Opportunity

Organizers with the Department of State's Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs called on Mason professor Arthur Romano to conduct a workshop on how to turn the musical art of hip-hop DJs, MCs, beatmakers, and dancers from around the world into tools for conflict resolution.

State Department Says Mason Professor's Got the Beat

Driven to Serve

For Joshua Richter, becoming a military doctor is another extension of his call to serve. The U.S. Army Sergeant, who assisted military doctors in Afghanistan and Iraq, is part of the first graduating class of the Enlisted to Medical Degree Preparatory Program at George Mason University's College of Science.

Heeding the Call a Second Time

Focused On Your Future

George Mason University psychology doctoral student Afra Ahmad studied how religious discrimination claims have changed in response to a nation's religious diversity, unemployment rates, and voting behaviors, and how that influences experiences on the job. "Workplace experiences matter so much in shaping our well-being, happiness, the way we see the world, everything," she says.
