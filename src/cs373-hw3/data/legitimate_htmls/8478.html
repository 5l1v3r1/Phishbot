  • Final Overtime Rule Announced
    The U.S. Department of Labor released a rule change today that will make millions of exempt employees eligible for overtime pay. Last night, the White House announced many of the changes businesses will soon be facing. Learn more about the rule and its effects here. 
  • Overtime Rule Issued; Salary Level to
    Increase Every Three Years
    The Department of Labor issued the long-anticipated final overtime rule that includes an exempt employee threshold of $47,476—less than the proposed rule's $50,440 but slightly more than double the old threshold of $23,660.
  • Overtime Changes Will Affect Employee Benefits, Too
    The overtime regulations overhaul will not only alter pay structures but will push some organizations to make benefit cuts. Employers should revisit their total rewards mix and prepare to deal with pay compression issues.
