    Million Dollars, But... The Game

    9 hours ago

    One of our favorite things about making the show Million Dollars, But... is the day that we get to sit around at the bar and debate the ridiculous scenarios we have concocted. The heart of the show is the "clarifying round" where people get to ask questions about their idea to really figure if they would do it. And now, we have a way for you to experience that as well. I am proud to announce our first ever card game: Million Dollars, But... The Game, which goes live on Kickstarter at 12:01 p.m. CT today.


    Ever since Lazer Team, I have been trying to find a way for us to recreate the awesome experience of our crowdfunding campaign. It was such an incredible community experience that ran for 30 days, but lasted for over a year. It made our first-ever feature film possible, and created unique experiences like theatrical screenings and the "Infamous 535."


    Our first step into the world of tabletop gaming seems like a perfect fit. Our goals are much lower this time around (cards cost less than movies), but it's a great way to introduce our game to the world. And we have some really cool social games planned for the stretch goals, which we will announce once the dust from our launch settles. We even got to make one of those ridiculously pretentious announcement videos.



    Thanks for your support. I hope you love the game as much as we have loved designing it. #ButStuff

    Am I the Last One Posting to This Forum Dedicated to Pimp My Ride: The Video Game?

    2 days ago

    By @thegeoffjohnston


    Hey, where did everyone go?


    One minute we’re all hanging out on this lively message board, having a grand old time discussing the pros and cons of Pimp My Ride: The Video Game, and then all of a sudden this place is an absolute ghost town! What gives, you guys?


    I know I wasn’t the only one taking part in routine lively debates about how to best assist rapper/host Xzibit on his mission to revitalize the collective automotive landscape of the fictional pixelated Pimp City and turn it into a garishly colored wonderland of flamboyant sports cars and tricked out, reinvigorated late-model sedans while not running afoul of rival detailers and body shop bad guys. I mean, Pimp City is lousy with poorly maintained buckets and beaters, and it was our job to upgrade these rotted out hoopties into the wildest whips on the block. And for a while there it looked like we were all on board. I guess that just isn’t the case these days.


    Look, I know that the game has been out for almost 10 years. Yes, I’m fully aware that it has a collective Metascore of 38. And fine, at the time Official Xbox Magazine unflatteringly compared it to a game that recreates the experience of being a proctologist. But that didn’t stop us from coming to this forum and posting about our many accomplishments and achievements on a regular basis. But it looks like everyone has fled for greener pastures with better frame rates and less shitty mini-challenges. It would appear as though I’m on my own now. But I’ll be good and god dammed if it’s going to stop me from regularly posting reports regarding my ongoing progress to the forum.


    Why, just last week I was able to convert a rust-riddled unicycle into a platinum-plated double-decker party limo with neon spinners decked out with not one, not two, but FOUR stripper poles securely mounted within the passenger bay. I bet you didn’t even know you could do that! I sure didn’t. That is, until I did in fact do it. That’s what Pimp My Ride: The Video Game is all about. It is more than a game. It is a weaver of limitless dreams. It is a testament to the payoff of perseverance. It is the reason gaming was invented: so we could reach deep within ourselves and make precious gemstones out of filthy fistfuls of gravel and soot.

    So, you guys may have moved on. That’s fine. I know the game is flawed. I know it’s repetitious. You think I enjoyed spending six long weeks smashing into the thousands of opponent cars that would inexplicably earn me the cash to afford an ice sculpture of a 40-foot swan and a fully functioning Jacuzzi to sufficiently glitz out my purple and teal Hummer limo? I missed my sister’s wedding for that!


    But I’m not even done. There are even more challenges I have to master. I have yet to find the funds and build the skills to pimp out a 1973 Chevy Nova into a Mardi Gras float that can accommodate an authentic 32-piece New Orleans jazz band, trick out a small child’s scooter into a nitro-fueled street racer capable of outrunning law enforcement, and complete all the side quests, which mostly have to do with a particularly bizarre series of meetings with Xzibit’s attorneys regarding allegations of tax evasion.

    These remaining challenges are not going to be easy. But I’m not easily deterred. I’m seeing this through, dammit. Even if it kills me, I am going to utterly dominate this terrible fucking game that was clearly developed by a cabal of cocaine-fueled demons.


    Still, it brings me a certain amount of joy and satisfaction to report that not only has my incessant, decade-long dedication to Pimp My Ride: The Video Game helped pay for itself in terms of enthusiasm and a semi-legitimate excuse from dealing with the outside world – it has also paid for all of your purchases and reckless, orphaned gameplay. You are welcome very much. Still, I wish you the best of luck as you chase down the latest games, only to abandon them with the rapid progression of trends and time. Have fun in your Dark Souls III forum. If you need me, you’ll know where to find me. I’ll be in Pimp City, helping my old friend Xzibit transform an old timey horse and buggy into an all-terrain hovercraft armed with laser-guided confetti cannons.

    Day 5 coming June 19th

    2 days ago

    Rooster Teeth is finally getting into the drama game with the debut of our new series "Day 5." Consisting of six hour-long episodes, "Day 5" is set to premiere on RoosterTeeth.com on June 19. The series stars Jesse C. Boyd ("The Choice," "The Wanderers"), Stephanie Drapeau ("Startup," "Finding Carter"), Davi Jay ("True Detective," "Walking Dead"), and Walker Satterwhite ("My All American").


    Here's a summary, for those who are still in the dark:

    Day 5 is the first dramatic series from Rooster Teeth Productions, set in the immediate aftermath of a fatal sleep epidemic. After a fortuitous drug bender saves his life, addict Jake (Jesse C. Boyd) ventures out into the quiet streets… unaware that most of the world already died in their beds. Now, battling sleepless fatigue and encroaching delirium, Jake teams with a scrappy teenager (Walker Satterwhite), overnight doctor (Stephanie Drapeau) and red-eye pilot (Davi Jay) to search for answers… and just maybe find a way to sleep again. Set in a world of insomniacs, late-shift workers and roving psychotics, Day 5 presents a unique vision of the apocalypse that fuses serial drama and thriller around a human story of survival and redemption. Day 5 premieres June 19th at RoosterTeeth.com.


    We can't wait for you to see it. Make sure you watch the trailer, and we'll see you June 19th when it all begins.
