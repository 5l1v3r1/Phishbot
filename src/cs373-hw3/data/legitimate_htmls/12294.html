How to write a CV
People generally underestimate how important a well written CV is. It's a candidates first contact with a prospective employer and it's important to remember that when it comes to your CV looks really do matter. Essentially your CV is a passport to getting a job interview.

A well written CV can make you VISIBLE, a bad one can make you invisible.

The fundamental points to writing a good CV are:

  • Identifying what an employer wants from a candidate.
  • Identifying what you can offer the employer. 
  • Writing a good CV that matches the above two points as closely as possible.

Finding the rights words is often one of the hardest parts of writing a good curriculum vitae, get round this problem by downloading and viewing our free CV samples. These will show you how to avoid over generalized career statements, compliment potential employers on their reputation and also keep your CV to a maximum of two pages [in a CV less is more].
