  • Professor Alan Kin-tak Lau elected to European think tank

    Professor Alan Kin-tak Lau, Pro-Vice-Chancellor (Research Performance and Development) at Swinburne University of Technology has been elected a Member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts.

    The Academy is one of the biggest think tanks in Europe, connecting about 1800 scientists worldwide, among them 32 Nobel Prize winners. To date only three academics in Australia have received this honour.

    Professor Lau recently joined Swinburne to oversee research performance and drive rankings development, engagement and improvement activities.

    He is an internationally recognised researcher having achieved around 9000 citations with an h-index of 45. His specialist research area is on bio-composite, smart composite and nano-composite materials.

  • Swinburne partners with Vietnamese university

    Swinburne has formalised its first research partnership in Vietnam, with International University, Ho Chi Minh City.

    International University is an esteemed institution in Vietnam. It is one of six members of the Vietnam National University, Ho Chi Minh City, and is the first public university in Vietnam to use English as the primary language in teaching and research.

    The partnership will bring together researchers at International University and Swinburne to offer a partnered PhD agreement in the areas of business and biotechnology. It will also allow leading experts from both universities to collaborate on new research projects in designated fields.

    Read more

    Swinburne has created a professional development program for academic staff from some of our Chinese international partner institutions. Using online modules and face to face workshops, the program will enhance teaching skills through curriculum development and design, ability to teach students research skills, and building awareness of leadership.

  • Swinburne and Nawaloka College of Higher Studies partner with Cal State University, Monterey Bay

    Students at Nawaloka College of Higher Studies (NCHS) in Sri Lanka can now study a Swinburne Foundation Year or UniLink diploma and continue to a related bachelor degree at the campus of their choice in one of three countries.

    The exclusive partnership between Swinburne University of Technology, NCHS, and California State University, Monterey Bay, USA, consists of a two way transfer program between the three educational providers. All commencing students will have an established pathway to a partner university and will be supported in the transition to their chosen campus in Melbourne (Australia), Kuching (Malaysia), or Monterey Bay (USA).

  • Swinburne - one of two universities in Victoria to be awarded a Laureate Fellowship.

    Swinburne University of Technology’s Pro Vice-Chancellor (Research) Professor Matthew Bailes has been awarded a prestigious Australian Laureate Fellowship, worth more than $2.8 million, by the federal government. Laureate Fellowships recognise outstanding leaders and fund them to pursue exciting research projects that develop research capacity and have the potential to make high-impact scientific breakthroughs.

    Professor Bailes’ fellowship will enable his team to transform the largest telescope in the southern hemisphere into a machine that will attempt to count the number of atoms in the Universe. The awards were announced by the Minister for Education and Training, Christopher Pyne. “The research programs of the 15 Laureate Fellows announced today will deliver outcomes that will benefit our nation, its economy and our people,” Mr Pyne said.

    Read more

  • Swinburne discovers new worlds using the Keck telescopes

    From a remote control room at Swinburne University of Technology’s Hawthorn campus, astronomers from the Centre for Astrophysics and Supercomputing have successfully steered the world’s largest optical telescopes more than 9000 kilometres away at the W M Keck Observatory in Hawaii. This has been made possible through an agreement between Swinburne University of Technology and the California Institute of Technology (Caltech).

    Over the past three years, direct access to Keck Observatory has enabled Swinburne astronomers to make discoveries such as the diamond planet, the emerald-cut galaxy, an ultra-compact dwarf galaxy, and more recently the discovery of a new supernova located in the outskirts of a galaxy some 100 million light years away.

    Read more

  • Cosmic radio burst caught red-handed

    Using CSIRO’s 64 metre Parkes radio telescope in eastern Australia, Swinburne University of Technology PhD student Emily Petroff has for the first time seen a ‘fast radio burst’ – a short, sharp flash of radio waves from an unknown source – happening live.

    “These bursts were generally discovered week, months or even more than a decade after they happened,” Ms Petroff said. “We are the first to catch one in real time.” She said identifying the origin of the fast radio bursts is now only a matter of time.

  • Swinburne secures $1.1 million in grants for optical IT research projects with China

    Researchers at Swinburne University of Technology’s Centre for Micro-Photonics (CMP) have been awarded $6.5 million CN¥ (A$1.1 million) by the National Natural Science Foundation of China for two information photonics (I-Photonics) research projects through established collaborations with two leading institutes in China, Beijing Institute of Technology (BIT) and Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics.   • Smart Structures Laboratory

    The Smart Structures Laboratory is paving the way for the next generation of structures and construction materials to be thoroughly tested and to provide industry and consumers with the necessary level of confidence in performance and safety.

    The $15 million laboratory forms part of the Centre for Sustainable Infrastructure (CSI) and is a major three-dimensional testing facility developed for large scale testing of civil, mechanical, aerospace and mining engineering components and systems. It is the only one of its type available in Australia.
